Join the Camp It Up! Team!

We are excited about the 2020 summer camp and are hiring for our program staff. There are several positions available and they are all essential to the magic that is Camp It Up! (CIU!). The positions are:
  • Senior Counselor
  • Junior Counselor
  • Counselor In Training (CIT)
  • Specialist
  • Program Staff Director
  • Program Director
  • CIT Director

At camp all staff members have two work shifts every day as well as a required daily morning meeting (8-9am). The three shifts are: 9AM-12PM, 1PM-5PM and 6PM-10PM. There will also be some additional shifts as needed, though these might not be 3-4 hours long.

CIT's might only have one shift some days and two on other days, depending on their trainings. Director hours may vary from this.

CIU! has a zero drug and alcohol policy while in camp, even if you're holding a medical card, or over 21.

Applications must be sent in by April 1st to be considered for a staff position. Any qualified applicants who apply after April 1st will be considered only on an as needed basis. We understand that new outgoing college students might not know until May 1st, so if that's the case and you want to still be considered, you still need to apply by April 1st but please include the details in the notes section of the application. The job descriptions are below as well as the link to the applications. LGBTQI+ and People of Color are encouraged to apply.

Openings for 2020

Senior Counselor
Requirements:
  • Must be 21 years or older
  • Must have leadership skills and be creative
  • Must have experience working with children/youth/teens
  • Must have experience working in a group
Job Description:
A Senior Counselor is the head counselor within a children, youth or teen's group.
Main responsibilities include:
  • focusing on the entire group dynamic
  • creating a dynamic and working group schedule group culture
  • establishing a group camp culture
  • leading and communicating to other counselors in your group the activities for the day, assessing needs, and adjusting accordingly.
  • updating the camper communications board with a daily schedule and what campers need to bring.
  • filling out an Incident Report when needed and delivering it to the Administrative Director
  • knowing the special needs, allergies and medical needs of all children/youth/teens in their group and communicating this to your group. This information will be provided by the Administrative Director.
  • being a mentor and support to the CIT.
  • establishing a group camp culture
  • reporting all issues that arise in group to the Executive Director, Program Staff Director and CIT Director
  • asking for support from necessary Specialists
  • keeping parents/guardians informed of how their children/youth/teens are doing in group.
Junior Counselor
Requirements:
  • Must be 18 years or older
  • Must have leadership skills and be creative
  • Must have experience working with children/youth/teens
  • Must have experience working in a group
Job Description:
A Junior Counselor’s main role is to support the Senior Counselor within your group and be the second leader for the group.
Main responsibilities include:
  • working closely with your Senior Counselor to help develop the group program for the week
  • keeping focus on individual group members and supporting each child/youth/teen
  • being a mentor and support to the CIT
Counselor in Training (CIT)
Requirements:
  • Must be at least 16 or have completed one year of high school (exceptions will be considered at the Executive Director's discretion)
  • Leadership skills and creativity
  • Clear communication of your strengths, weaknesses and hopes with the Executive Director, Program Staff Director and CIT Director
  • Be open to camp job placements
  • Attend required daily meetings for both all-staff and CIT specific
  • Participate in pre-camp trainings and meetings
Job Description:
As a CIT, you are expected to participate in a two to three years of of leadership training at Camp It Up!. The training program is led by the CIT Director with support from the Program Staff Director or Executive Director. If you are placed in a group, you will be working directly with the Senior and Junior Counselors of your group. If you are placed as or with a Specialist or with program/administration, you will be working directly with Program or Administrative Directors.
Specialists (Kids and Adults)
Requirements:
  • Must be 18 years or older
  • Must have leadership skills and be creative
  • Must have experience working with children/youth/teens and/or adults
  • Must have experience working in a group
  • A specific skill you can share and teach
Job Description:
Specialists are needed are for Drama/Dance, Administration, Music, Children/youth/teen Arts and Crafts, Adult Arts and Crafts (tye-dye, pine-needle baskets, silk painting skills are a plus), Specialty Crafts (mosaics, carpentry skills are a plus) and Sports.
As a Specialist, you will follow the leadership of the Director team.
Main responsibilities include:
  • working with a Director on all camp programming, planning, set up and break down
  • creating a curriculum or program plan
  • coordinating with the Program Director for all needed supplies for ordering by June 15.
  • communicatinge with the Program Staff Director the availability and details about your program
  • communicatinge with Administrative Director and Hospitality Coordinator to make sign upsign-up sheets for your programs and track payments and fees owed if required.
Program Staff Director
Requirements:
  • Must be 25 years or older
  • Must have leadership skills
  • Must be creative and flexible
  • Must have great collaboration and communication skills
  • Must be organized and well directed
  • Must have experience working with children/youth/teens and/or adults
  • Must have experience working in a group and team building
Job Description:
The Program Staff Director’s main responsibilities include:
  • working with the Executive Director, Program Director and CIT Director to plan, implement, and break-down all family and evening programing
  • assisting in supervising staff
  • living in the Staff Village and leading night and evening supervison
  • working with all Specialists to support the production of the Camp Show and Talent Show, art projects, etc.
  • working with Specialists and program staff to bring afternoon and evening programming.
  • leading implementation of "Pizzazz" or decoration of camp and camp programs.
